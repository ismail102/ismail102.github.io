Bio

Currently, Ismail Hossain is a Ph.D. student at SIU Carbondale. He earned his Masters degree in big data analytics from University of Illinois. His specialization includes Big data, Machine Learning, Parallel Processing and Cloud Services. In his past position, he worked as a data scientist, where he was part of some of the prestigious products like Google CP. In his experience with IT, he worked across different domains that include Financial services, Retail, Cloud services, Transportation and Manufacturing industries. Also, he is a certified solutions architect and data engineer. His fascination and curiosity towards big data and machine learning ignited a spark inside him to explore more beyond the horizon. He worked with different languages such as C, C++, Java, .Net, Python, Hive, Hadoop, Spark, NoSQL, SQL Server, Oracle, R, Impala, Hive, HBase, SAS, Tableau, Perl etc. Additionally, he has certifications in Big Data Analytics from Columbia, MIT, Harvard, Microsoft and IIT-Hyderabad. His research explores the interplay between big data, networking, security, and machine learning adaptability. Specifically, he aims to understand and resolve the tensions between big data and security with machine learning and deep learning strategies that govern its eventual real-world context.

Recent Achievements
  • A team of graduate students from the computer science program recently placed highly in a national cybersecurity contest

    The CyberSalukis placed 16th overall during the U.S. Department of Energy’s CyberForce Competition, held late in 2022.

    The DOE competition is aimed at building students’ critical infrastructure and cyber security nexus knowledge and awareness of hands-on cyber education. It also aims to increase fundamental understanding of cyber security within a real-world context. The CyberForce competition included a range of cybersecurity activities related to a renewable energy scenario revolving around a fictitious electric vehicle manufacturing plant and its solar power generation facility.

    More than 140 teams from 108 institutions across country participated in the competition. The SIU School of Computing team, CyberSalukis, finished 4th among the 12 participating teams from Illinois, while another SIU team, Security Dawgs, secured 41st place in the competition overall.
