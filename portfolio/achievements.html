3rd Place — Miners Pitch Competition, UTEP

Secured 3rd place out of 47 applicants in the Spring 2026 Miners Pitch Competition, organized by the Mike Loya Center for Innovation and Commerce and Arrowhead Center at UTEP. The competition was a month-long program encompassing orientation, three workshops, and individual mentoring sessions focused on transforming research into real-world impact.

Presented the research project "AI-in-the-Loop" as a 5-minute business pitch — covering the problem, solution, proof, market opportunity, and business model. The idea was among the top three selected, and has also been accepted at PoPETs 2026. The award was presented by Dr. Heather Wilson, President of The University of Texas at El Paso.

Grateful to advisors Daniel Ziesmer and Jaime Bari (Arrowhead Center), mentor Carlos Benitez, program organizers Dr. Nicole Aguilar and Karla Ramirez (Mike Loya Center), workshop guide Ben Hokenson, and research advisor Prof. Sajedul Talukder, Ph.D. April 24, 2026

A team of graduate students from the computer science program placed highly in a national cybersecurity contest. The CyberSalukis placed 16th overall during the U.S. Department of Energy's CyberForce Competition, held late in 2022. The competition aims to build students' critical-infrastructure and cyber-security knowledge through hands-on cyber education within a real-world context, revolving around a fictitious electric-vehicle manufacturing plant and its solar power generation facility. More than 140 teams from 108 institutions across the country participated; the SIU School of Computing team, CyberSalukis, finished 4th among the 12 participating teams from Illinois, while another SIU team, Security Dawgs, secured 41st place overall.
